Emergence

Eight models of collective behaviour. Each starts with simple local rules between agents that know nothing of the whole. Each produces complex global patterns that no agent intended. The intelligence is in the interaction, not the individual.

I

Cellular Automata

Discrete cells on a grid. Each cell looks at its neighbours and decides: alive or dead. That's all.
Emerges: gliders, oscillators, spaceships, universal computation. Conway proved a grid of on/off cells following a two-line rule can compute anything.
II

Lenia

Continuous cells, smooth kernels, floating-point states. The discrete grid dissolved into a continuous field.
Emerges: organisms that grow, move, reproduce, and die. Some hunt, some graze, some pulse. Artificial life from a differential equation.
III

Fluid Dynamics

Conservation of mass and momentum. Pressure pushes, viscosity drags, advection carries. The Navier-Stokes equations.
Emerges: vortices, turbulence, Kármán streets, laminar-to-turbulent transitions. The unsolved mathematics of why cream swirls in coffee.
IV

Reaction-Diffusion

Two chemicals. One activates, one inhibits. Both diffuse. The ratio of their diffusion rates determines everything.
Emerges: spots, stripes, spirals, coral, fingerprints. Turing's 1952 morphogenesis paper — the mechanism behind animal markings.
V

Particle Life

Coloured particles. A random matrix says how much each colour attracts or repels every other. Repel close, attract medium, ignore far.
Emerges: cells, chains, orbits, predator-prey cycles, symbiosis. A 6×6 matrix produces ecosystems. Change one number, collapse a civilisation.
VI

Boids

Three rules: don't crowd nearby birds, steer toward their heading, steer toward their centre. No leader. No plan.
Emerges: flocking, murmuration, predator evasion. A thousand agents that look individually stupid produce collectively intelligent movement.
VII

Wave Function Collapse

Each cell holds a superposition of possibilities. Observe one, and its neighbours' possibilities narrow. Constraint propagation ripples outward.
Emerges: coherent maps, dungeons, circuits, cities — all from local adjacency rules. The bridge between noise and design. Structure from constraint, not instruction.
VIII

Evolution

Creatures with neural networks sense food and each other. Genes mutate on reproduction. Energy determines survival. Fitness is emergent, not designed.
Emerges: adaptation, speciation, arms races, niche differentiation — natural selection as computation. The only optimiser that works without understanding the problem.

The same principle in eight forms: simple agents, local rules, no central controller → complex collective behaviour.
